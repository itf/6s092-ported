# L03: Regularized Solutions

Tikhonov regularization addresses the ill-posedness of learning by adding a smoothness penalty. Instead of minimizing empirical risk alone, we minimize $H[f] = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n V(f(x_i), y_i) + \lambda \|f\|_{\mathcal{H}}^2$, where $\lambda > 0$ is the regularization parameter and $\|\cdot\|_{\mathcal{H}}$ is a norm in a suitable function space. The regularization term encodes prior knowledge about the smoothness of the target function.

## Tikhonov Regularization

The Tikhonov functional has the form:
$$H_\lambda[f] = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n (f(x_i) - y_i)^2 + \lambda \|Df\|^2$$
where $D$ is a differential operator that measures "roughness." The minimizer trades off fit to data against smoothness.

<checkyourself>
What happens to the regularized solution as $\lambda \to 0$ and as $\lambda \to \infty$?

<showhide>
As $\lambda \to 0$: the regularization penalty becomes negligible and the solution approaches the unregularized interpolant (potentially overfitting). As $\lambda \to \infty$: the penalty dominates and the solution becomes very smooth, eventually approaching a constant function (the mean of the $y_i$ values), which underfits the data.
</showhide>
</checkyourself>

## Representer Theorem

A fundamental result is the **Representer Theorem**: for a wide class of regularization functionals defined in a Reproducing Kernel Hilbert Space (RKHS), the minimizer of $H_\lambda[f]$ has the form:
$$f^*(x) = \sum_{i=1}^n c_i K(x, x_i)$$
where $K$ is the kernel associated with the RKHS. This reduces the infinite-dimensional optimization to a finite-dimensional one.

<checkyourself>
Why does adding $\lambda I$ to the Gram matrix $K$ (or $X^\top X$) help with numerical stability?

<showhide>
The matrix $K$ (or $X^\top X$) may be singular or ill-conditioned, making the system hard to solve. Adding $\lambda I$ shifts all eigenvalues up by $\lambda$, making the smallest eigenvalue at least $\lambda > 0$. This ensures the matrix is positive definite and well-conditioned, stabilizing the solution.
</showhide>
</checkyourself>
