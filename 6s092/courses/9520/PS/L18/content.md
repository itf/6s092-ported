# Math Camp: Functional Analysis and Probability Theory

This lecture reviews key mathematical tools underlying the course: functional analysis (Hilbert spaces, operators, spectral theory) and probability theory (concentration inequalities, convergence). These tools are the mathematical foundation for RKHS theory, stability analysis, and generalization bounds.

A **Hilbert space** $\mathcal{H}$ is a complete inner product space. Key examples: $L^2(\mathcal{X}, \mu)$ (square-integrable functions), $\ell^2$ (square-summable sequences), and RKHS. **Compact operators** on Hilbert spaces have a spectral decomposition analogous to matrix diagonalization: $T = \sum_i \mu_i \langle \cdot, \phi_i \rangle \phi_i$ where $\mu_i \to 0$. The **integral operator** $T_K f(x) = \int K(x,t) f(t)\, d\mu(t)$ is compact and self-adjoint, yielding Mercer's theorem.

Key **concentration inequalities** used in the course: **Markov's** ($P(X \geq a) \leq \mathbb{E}[X]/a$), **Chebyshev's** ($P(|X-\mu| \geq k\sigma) \leq 1/k^2$), **Hoeffding's** ($P(|\bar{X}-\mu| \geq \epsilon) \leq 2\exp(-2n\epsilon^2/M^2)$ for bounded r.v.s), and **McDiarmid's** (bounded differences, used for stability bounds).

## Hilbert Spaces and Operators

<checkyourself>
State the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality in a Hilbert space and give one application in learning theory.

<showhide>
**Cauchy-Schwarz:** $|\langle f, g \rangle_\mathcal{H}| \leq \|f\|_\mathcal{H} \|g\|_\mathcal{H}$ for all $f, g \in \mathcal{H}$, with equality iff $f$ and $g$ are proportional. **Application:** In RKHS, the reproducing property gives $f(x) = \langle f, K(\cdot,x)\rangle_\mathcal{H}$. By Cauchy-Schwarz: $|f(x)| \leq \|f\|_\mathcal{H} \|K(\cdot,x)\|_\mathcal{H} = \|f\|_\mathcal{H}\sqrt{K(x,x)}$. This is the key bound used in the stability proof for Tikhonov regularization.
</showhide>

<checkyourself>
Compare the tightness of Markov, Chebyshev, and Hoeffding inequalities for bounding $P(|X - \mu| \geq \epsilon)$ when $X$ is bounded. Which is tightest and why?

<showhide>
**Markov:** $P(X \geq a) \leq \mathbb{E}[X]/a$ — very weak, uses only the mean, gives $O(1/\epsilon)$ tail decay. **Chebyshev:** $P(|X-\mu| \geq k\sigma) \leq 1/k^2$ — uses variance, gives $O(1/\epsilon^2)$ tail decay. **Hoeffding:** $P(|\bar{X}-\mu| \geq \epsilon) \leq 2\exp(-2n\epsilon^2/M^2)$ — exponential tail decay for averages of bounded r.v.s, much tighter. Hoeffding is tightest for the mean of bounded i.i.d. variables because it exploits both boundedness and independence to give sub-Gaussian concentration.
</showhide>
</checkyourself>
