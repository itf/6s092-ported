# Boosting and Bagging

**Ensemble methods** combine multiple weak learners to form a strong classifier. **Bagging** (Bootstrap Aggregating) reduces variance: train $T$ classifiers on $T$ bootstrap samples (sampling $n$ points with replacement), then average predictions. **Boosting** reduces bias by training classifiers sequentially, with each focusing on the errors of previous ones.

**AdaBoost** maintains a distribution $D_t$ over training examples. At round $t$: find weak learner $h_t$ minimizing weighted error $\epsilon_t = \sum_i D_t(i)\mathbf{1}[h_t(x_i) \neq y_i]$; set $\alpha_t = \frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{1-\epsilon_t}{\epsilon_t}$; update $D_{t+1}(i) \propto D_t(i)\exp(-\alpha_t y_i h_t(x_i))$. The final classifier is $H(x) = \text{sign}\left(\sum_t \alpha_t h_t(x)\right)$.

The **training error** of AdaBoost decreases exponentially: $\frac{1}{n}\sum_i \mathbf{1}[H(x_i)\neq y_i] \leq \prod_t \sqrt{4\epsilon_t(1-\epsilon_t)} = \prod_t \sqrt{1 - 4\gamma_t^2}$, where $\gamma_t = 1/2 - \epsilon_t > 0$ is the edge. If $\gamma_t \geq \gamma > 0$ for all $t$, training error drops as $(1-4\gamma^2)^{T/2}$, which is exponentially small in $T$.

## Bias-Variance Tradeoff

<checkyourself>
Explain the difference between bias and variance in the context of ensemble methods. Which does bagging reduce and which does boosting reduce?

<showhide>
**Bias** is the systematic error due to a model being too simple (underfitting). **Variance** is the error due to sensitivity to training data (overfitting). **Bagging** averages over many models, reducing variance without changing bias (averaging reduces fluctuations). **Boosting** sequentially reduces residual errors, primarily reducing bias (and can increase variance if overfitted).

State the AdaBoost training error bound. Under what condition on the weak learners does AdaBoost achieve exponentially small training error?

<showhide>
The bound is: training error $\leq \prod_t \sqrt{1 - 4\gamma_t^2}$, where $\gamma_t = 1/2 - \epsilon_t$ is the edge of weak learner $t$. If there exists $\gamma > 0$ such that all weak learners have edge $\gamma_t \geq \gamma$ ("weak learning condition"), then training error $\leq (1-4\gamma^2)^{T/2}$, which decays exponentially in $T$. This requires each weak learner to do slightly better than random (error $< 1/2$).
