# Network Flow / Max-Flow Min-Cut

Network flow is a fundamental combinatorial optimization problem with applications to matching, scheduling, and transportation. The max-flow min-cut theorem is one of the most elegant results in combinatorics.

## Ford-Fulkerson Algorithm

The Ford-Fulkerson algorithm repeatedly finds an augmenting path from $s$ to $t$ in the **residual graph** and pushes as much flow along it as possible. The residual graph has a forward edge with capacity $c(u,v) - f(u,v)$ and a backward edge with capacity $f(u,v)$ for each original edge $(u,v)$.

## Max-Flow Min-Cut Theorem

**Theorem:** In any flow network, the maximum $s$-$t$ flow equals the minimum $s$-$t$ cut capacity.

An $s$-$t$ cut is a partition $(S, T)$ of vertices with $s \in S, t \in T$; its capacity is $\sum_{u \in S, v \in T} c(u,v)$.

<checkyourself>
Prove the weak duality part of max-flow min-cut: the value of any $s$-$t$ flow is at most the capacity of any $s$-$t$ cut.

<showhide>
Let $f$ be any $s$-$t$ flow with value $|f|$, and let $(S,T)$ be any $s$-$t$ cut. By flow conservation, for every internal node $v \in S \setminus \{s\}$, the net flow out equals zero. Summing over all nodes in $S$, the net flow out of $S$ equals the flow value $|f|$. The net flow out of $S$ equals $\sum_{u \in S, v \in T} f(u,v) - \sum_{u \in S, v \in T} f(v,u) \leq \sum_{u \in S, v \in T} c(u,v) = \text{cap}(S,T)$. So $|f| \leq \text{cap}(S,T)$ for any cut.
</showhide>
</checkyourself>
