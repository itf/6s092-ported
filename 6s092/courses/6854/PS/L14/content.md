# LP Relaxations for Approximation Algorithms

LP relaxations provide lower bounds on combinatorial problems and, combined with rounding, yield approximation algorithms with provable guarantees.

## Vertex Cover LP Relaxation

The **vertex cover LP** relaxes the 0/1 constraint:
$$\min \sum_v w_v x_v \quad \text{s.t.} \quad x_u + x_v \geq 1 \;\forall (u,v) \in E, \quad 0 \leq x_v \leq 1 \;\forall v$$

Rounding: take all vertices with $x_v^* \geq 1/2$. This gives a **2-approximation**.

What is the **integrality gap** of the vertex cover LP, and why does it prevent us from achieving a better than 2-approximation via LP rounding?

<showhide>
The integrality gap is the worst-case ratio between the LP optimum and the integer optimum: $\sup \frac{\text{OPT}_{LP}}{\text{OPT}_{IP}}$. For vertex cover, the integrality gap is exactly 2, achieved by complete graphs $K_n$ (LP optimum = $n/2$, integer optimum = $n-1$) or by odd cycles. Since LP rounding gives an approximation ratio of at most $\frac{\text{rounded cost}}{\text{OPT}_{IP}} \leq \frac{\text{rounded cost}}{\text{OPT}_{LP}} \cdot \text{gap}$, an integrality gap of 2 means LP rounding cannot achieve better than 2 without additional ideas.

## Set Cover LP Relaxation

The set cover LP: $\min \sum_S w_S x_S$ s.t. $\sum_{S \ni e} x_S \geq 1$ for all elements $e$, $x_S \geq 0$. Rounding: include set $S$ if $x_S^* \geq 1/(f)$ where $f$ is the maximum frequency (elements per set)—this gives an $f$-approximation.
