# Online Learning and Regret Minimization

Online learning studies algorithms that make sequential decisions without knowing the future, aiming to minimize regret compared to the best fixed strategy in hindsight.

## Regret and No-Regret Learning

**Regret** of an online algorithm after $T$ rounds: $R_T = \sum_{t=1}^T \ell_t(x_t) - \min_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \sum_{t=1}^T \ell_t(x)$.

An algorithm has **no regret** if $R_T / T \to 0$ as $T \to \infty$.

## Follow the Regularized Leader (FTRL)

FTRL at round $t$ plays: $x_t = \arg\min_{x \in \mathcal{X}} \left[\sum_{s<t} \ell_s(x) + R(x)\right]$ where $R(x)$ is a regularizer (e.g., $\|x\|^2 / \eta$ or negative entropy $\sum_i x_i \ln x_i$).

<checkyourself>
Explain why Follow the Leader (FTL, with no regularization) can fail badly on adversarial inputs, and how regularization fixes this.

<showhide>
FTL: play $x_t = \arg\min_x \sum_{s<t} \ell_s(x)$. Adversarial failure: suppose $\mathcal{X} = \{0,1\}$ (two actions), and the adversary alternates $\ell_t(x) = x$ then $\ell_t(x) = 1-x$. FTL always plays the action that was better in the past, exactly the wrong choice each time, incurring $T$ loss vs best action's $T/2$ loss. Regularization (FTRL) smooths decisions by adding $R(x)/\eta$: large regularization makes $x_t$ change slowly (stability), at the cost of being biased toward the regularizer's minimum. The regret bound $O(\sqrt{T})$ balances stability vs adaptivity.
</showhide>
