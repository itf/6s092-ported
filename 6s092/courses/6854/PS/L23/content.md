# Sparse Recovery and L1 Minimization

$\ell_1$ minimization (basis pursuit) is the algorithmic heart of compressed sensing—it recovers sparse signals efficiently and is robust to noise.

## Basis Pursuit Recovery Guarantee

**Theorem (Candès, 2008):** If $A$ satisfies RIP with $\delta_{2k} < \sqrt{2} - 1 \approx 0.414$, then $\ell_1$ minimization $\min \|x\|_1$ s.t. $Ax = b$ recovers any $k$-sparse $x^*$ exactly.

The constant $\sqrt{2}-1$ is known to be tight.

## Noise-Robust Recovery

For noisy measurements $b = Ax^* + e$ with $\|e\|_2 \leq \sigma$, we solve:
$$\hat{x} = \arg\min \|x\|_1 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \|Ax - b\|_2 \leq \sigma$$

<checkyourself>
In the noisy setting, why can we not simply solve $Ax = b$ exactly? What does the noise-robust recovery guarantee look like?

<showhide>
With noise, $b = Ax^* + e$ so $x^*$ is not feasible for $Ax = b$. Instead we use a relaxed constraint $\|Ax-b\| \leq \sigma$. The guarantee is: if $A$ satisfies RIP with $\delta_{2k} < \sqrt{2}-1$, then $\|\hat{x} - x^*\|_2 \leq C \sigma$ for some constant $C$ depending on the RIP constant. More precisely, even if $x^*$ is not exactly $k$-sparse, we get $\|\hat{x}-x^*\|_2 \leq C_1 \sigma + C_2 \frac{\|x^* - x^*_k\|_1}{\sqrt{k}}$ where $x^*_k$ is the best $k$-sparse approximation.
</showhide>
