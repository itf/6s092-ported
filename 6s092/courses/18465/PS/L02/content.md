# Lecture 2: Boosting — Training Error Bounds

AdaBoost is an algorithm that builds a strong classifier from weak classifiers by iteratively reweighting training examples. At each round $t$, a weak classifier $h_t$ is chosen and weights are updated to emphasize misclassified examples. The key result is a bound showing that the training error decreases exponentially in the number of boosting rounds.

## AdaBoost Algorithm

AdaBoost initializes uniform weights $w_1(i) = 1/n$. At each step $t$:
1. Find classifier $h_t \in H$ with weighted error $\varepsilon_t = \sum_i w_t(i) \mathbf{1}[h_t(X_i) \neq Y_i]$.
2. Set $\alpha_t = \frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{1-\varepsilon_t}{\varepsilon_t} > 0$ (since $\varepsilon_t < 1/2$).
3. Update: $w_{t+1}(i) = \frac{w_t(i) e^{-\alpha_t Y_i h_t(X_i)}}{Z_t}$ where $Z_t$ normalizes.

Output: $f = \text{sign}\left(\sum_t \alpha_t h_t(x)\right)$.

<checkyourself>
Why does the weight update $w_{t+1}(i) \propto w_t(i) e^{-\alpha_t Y_i h_t(X_i)}$ emphasize misclassified examples?

<showhide>
When example $i$ is misclassified, $Y_i h_t(X_i) = -1$, so the exponent is $+\alpha_t > 0$, increasing the weight. When correctly classified, $Y_i h_t(X_i) = +1$, so the exponent is $-\alpha_t < 0$, decreasing the weight. This forces subsequent classifiers to focus on the harder examples.
</showhide>
</checkyourself>

## Training Error Bound

**Theorem:** Let $\gamma_t = 1/2 - \varepsilon_t > 0$ (the "edge" of $h_t$ over random). Then the training error satisfies:
$$\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n \mathbf{1}[f(X_i) \neq Y_i] \leq \prod_{t=1}^T \sqrt{1 - 4\gamma_t^2}.$$

Since $\sqrt{1-4\gamma_t^2} < 1$, this product decreases exponentially in $T$ when all $\gamma_t \geq \gamma > 0$.

What happens to the AdaBoost weight $\alpha_t$ when the weak classifier has error $\varepsilon_t$ approaching $0$ or approaching $1/2$?

<showhide>

As $\varepsilon_t \to 1/2$ (barely better than random), $\alpha_t \to 0$, giving essentially zero weight. The formula correctly scales influence by classifier quality.
