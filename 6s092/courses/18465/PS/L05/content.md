# Lecture 5: Concentration Inequalities â€” Bennett's Inequality

Concentration inequalities bound the probability that a sum of independent random variables deviates far from its mean. This lecture covers the foundational inequalities: Jensen's, Chebyshev's, Markov's, and Bennett's inequality. These tools are essential for bounding generalization error.

## Basic Inequalities

Let $Z_1, \ldots, Z_n$ be i.i.d. with mean $\mu = EZ$. We want to bound $P\left(\frac{1}{n}\sum Z_i - \mu \geq t\right)$.

- **Jensen's inequality:** For convex $\varphi$: $\varphi(EZ) \leq E\varphi(Z)$.
- **Markov/Chernoff:** For any $\lambda > 0$: $P(Z \geq t) \leq e^{-\lambda t} E e^{\lambda Z}$.
- **Chebyshev:** $P(Z \geq t) \leq EZ/t$ for $Z \geq 0$.

<checkyourself>
In the Chernoff/Markov bound, why do we optimize over $\lambda > 0$? What does this optimization achieve?

<showhide>
The bound $P(Z \geq t) \leq e^{-\lambda t} Ee^{\lambda Z}$ holds for every $\lambda > 0$. By minimizing over $\lambda$, we get the tightest possible bound of this form: $P(Z \geq t) \leq \inf_{\lambda > 0} e^{-\lambda t} Ee^{\lambda Z}$. The optimal $\lambda$ depends on $t$ and the distribution of $Z$.

## Bennett's Inequality

**Theorem (Bennett):** Let $EZ = 0$, $EZ^2 = \sigma^2$, $|Z| \leq M$, and $Z_1,\ldots,Z_n$ be i.i.d. copies. Then:
$$P\left(\sum_{i=1}^n Z_i \geq t\right) \leq \exp\left(-\frac{n\sigma^2}{M^2}\varphi\left(\frac{tM}{n\sigma^2}\right)\right),$$
where $\varphi(x) = (1+x)\log(1+x) - x$.

The function $\varphi(x) \approx x^2/2$ for small $x$ (recovering Gaussian-like bounds), and $\varphi(x) \approx x\log x$ for large $x$ (giving Poisson-like tails).
