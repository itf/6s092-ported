# Space Complexity and More

Beyond time, we can also measure the *space* (memory) a computation uses. **PSPACE** is the class of problems solvable with polynomial space (but potentially exponential time). **L** (logspace) uses only $O(\log n)$ workspace. The containment chain is:

$$P \subseteq NP \subseteq PSPACE \subseteq EXP$$

All containments are believed strict, but only $P \subsetneq EXP$ is proven (Time Hierarchy Theorem). PSPACE-complete problems include $n \times n$ chess and TQBF (True Quantified Boolean Formulas). Reingold (2005) showed that undirected graph connectivity is in L — a remarkable derandomization result.

**coNP** is the class of problems whose complements are in NP. Whether NP = coNP is a major open question; most believe they are different. **EXP** contains all problems solvable in $2^{p(n)}$ time for some polynomial $p$. The EXP upper bound for PSPACE follows because the number of distinct configurations of a polynomial-space machine is at most exponential.

## PSPACE and Game Complexity

$n \times n$ chess (with a polynomial move limit) is PSPACE-complete. Intuitively, game trees require alternating quantifiers ($\forall$ moves by White, $\exists$ moves by Black, ...), which is exactly what quantified Boolean formulas (QBF) capture. TQBF is PSPACE-complete: given $\forall x_1 \exists x_2 \forall x_3 \ldots \phi(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$, is the formula true?

## The Complexity Zoo

The complexity classes form a rich hierarchy. Notable classes include: **ZPP** (zero-error probabilistic P), **RP** (one-sided error), **BPP** (two-sided error, polynomial time), **IP** (interactive proofs, equals PSPACE), **PH** (polynomial hierarchy), and **#P** (counting problems). Understanding relationships between these classes is one of the central goals of TCS.
