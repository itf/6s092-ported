# Lecture 3: Concentration Inequalities and ERM with a Finite Class

This lecture analyzes the empirical risk minimizer (ERM) over a **finite hypothesis class** $\mathcal{H} = \{h_1, \ldots, h_M\}$. The main tool is **Hoeffding's inequality** for concentration of bounded random variables, which allows us to bound the excess risk of the ERM.

## Empirical Risk Minimization

Given data $(X_1, Y_1), \ldots, (X_n, Y_n)$, the empirical risk of $h$ is:
$$\hat{R}_n(h) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n \mathbf{1}(h(X_i) \neq Y_i).$$

The ERM $\hat{h}$ minimizes $\hat{R}_n(h)$ over $\mathcal{H}$. The oracle $\bar{h}$ minimizes the true risk $R(h)$ over $\mathcal{H}$.

## Main Theorem: ERM Bound for Finite Classes

**Theorem:** The ERM $\hat{h}$ satisfies, with probability at least $1 - \delta$:
$$R(\hat{h}) \leq R(\bar{h}) + \sqrt{\frac{2\log(2M/\delta)}{n}}.$$

In expectation:
$$\mathbb{E}[R(\hat{h})] \leq R(\bar{h}) + \sqrt{\frac{2\log(2M)}{n}}.$$

## Union Bound

The proof of the ERM bound applies a **union bound** over all $M$ classifiers in $\mathcal{H}$:
$$\mathbb{P}\left(\max_{j=1}^M |\hat{R}_n(h_j) - R(h_j)| \geq t\right) \leq \sum_{j=1}^M \mathbb{P}(|\hat{R}_n(h_j) - R(h_j)| \geq t) \leq 2M e^{-2nt^2}.$$

Setting $2Me^{-2nt^2} = \delta$ gives $t = \sqrt{\frac{\log(2M/\delta)}{2n}}$.
