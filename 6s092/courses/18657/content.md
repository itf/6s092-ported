## Course Description

This graduate-level course provides a mathematically rigorous introduction to machine learning, with a focus on statistical learning theory. The course treats machine learning as the automated identification of patterns in data, and studies both the methods and their theoretical analysis.

Key themes include:

- **Statistical Learning Theory:** Sample complexity, Bayes classifiers, empirical risk minimization
- **Concentration Inequalities:** Hoeffding, Bernstein, bounded differences
- **Complexity Theory for Learning:** VC dimension, Rademacher complexity, covering numbers, chaining
- **Convex Optimization:** Gradient descent, projected gradient descent, mirror descent, SGD
- **Online Learning:** Prediction with expert advice, Follow the Perturbed Leader, bandits
- **Game Theory:** Blackwell approachability
