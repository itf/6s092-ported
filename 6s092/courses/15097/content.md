# MIT 15.097: Prediction: Machine Learning and Statistics (Spring 2012)

**Instructor:** Prof. Cynthia Rudin (Sloan School of Management)

**[OCW Course Page](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/15-097-prediction-machine-learning-and-statistics-spring-2012/)**

## Course Description

This course focuses on prediction and generalization from data — the central problems in machine learning, statistics, and data mining. It synthesizes important topics from machine learning (developed within artificial intelligence over the last 30 years) and statistics, covering both theoretical foundations and practical algorithms.

Topics include: rule mining and the Apriori algorithm, fundamentals of supervised and unsupervised learning, clustering (k-means), k-nearest neighbors, Naïve Bayes, decision trees, logistic regression, boosting (AdaBoost), convex optimization and Lagrangian duality, support vector machines, kernel methods, statistical learning theory (VC dimension, generalization bounds), and Bayesian analysis.
